Resources

There are to many things to learn about modules for us to tell us everything you might need to know, but we have tried to gather several usefull links. Here you will find both technical specifications and inspiring Youtube videos.

There are several different module standards, some are compatible, others are not. Below you will find the link to three different standards used within N-skala V채st (listed from most to least common). A standards document can seem overwhelming at first, so don't dive to deep into it right away. Rather, browse around and look for inspiration and talk to other modelers. Then, when you are ready to start a build, return here and take a look. Often, a standards document does not specify what country or region to be inspired by, but focuses on what is needed to be sure modules from different builders connect well to one another.
Fremo n-Re The n-Re standard is used all over Europe, and is used for european railoading, regardless of specific country or region modeled..
Fremo americaN Similar to the n-Re standard but more suited for those who want to replicate North American Railroading. The most noticable difference from n-Re is the type of track used on the modules. It should be noted that models of european rolling stock (as well as older american models) won't run on americaN modules but engines and cars that runs on americaN modules will run on N-re Modules .
T-Trak T-Trak is very uncommon in Sweden but has quite a large following in the United States. The standard does not specify a specific country so it is up to the builders to decide. T-trak is often considered more toy-like than Fremo standards but that depends largely on how the modules are used. T-Trak can be more suitable for smaller layouts or home layouts, but there is nothing preventing a large group of modelers from filling an entire arena with modules.
