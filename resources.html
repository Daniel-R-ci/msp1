Resources

There are to many things to learn about modules for us to tell us everything you might need to know, but we have tried to gather several usefull links. Here you will find both technical specifications and inspiring Youtube videos.

There are several different module standards, some are compatible, others are not. Below you will find the link to three different standards used within N-skala Väst (listed from most to least common). A standards document can seem overwhelming at first, so don't dive to deep into it right away. Rather, browse around and look for inspiration and talk to other modelers. Then, when you are ready to start a build, return here and take a look. Often, a standards document does not specify what country or region to be inspired by, but focuses on what is needed to be sure modules from different builders connect well to one another.
Fremo n-Re
The n-Re standard is used all over Europe, and is used for european railoading, regardless of specific country or region modeled.
Fremo americaN
Similar to the n-Re standard but more suited for those who want to replicate North American Railroading. The most noticable difference from n-Re is the type of track used on the modules. It should be noted that models of european rolling stock (as well as older american models) won't run on americaN modules but engines and cars that runs on americaN modules will run on N-re Modules .
T-Trak
T-Trak is very uncommon in Sweden but has quite a large following in the United States. The standard does not specify a specific country so it is up to the builders to decide. T-trak is often considered more toy-like than Fremo standards but that depends largely on how the modules are used. T-Trak can be more suitable for smaller layouts or home layouts, but there is nothing preventing a large group of modelers from filling an entire arena with modules.

Here you will find links to different guides and videos that covers different aspects of module construction, both woodwork and scenery detailing. Does the thought of woodwork scare you? Don't worry, the first link is to a manufacturer selling module kits.
Pikart Modules
A Polish company selling module kits of several different standards. Many modelers, including members in N-Skala Väst, has used these kits instead of constructing their own. Just screw the parts together and start laying track.
SkånskaN
A group of n-scale modelers in southers Sweden has some very helpful guides when it comes to constructing your own modules.
Detail an n-scale module

Operating a small branch line

It may seem daunting at first, but we will help you into it and never leave you in a situation you are not comfortable with. In this Youtube video you can get a glimpse of what it means to make sure a specific car is delivered to the right customer and not just loop around around endlessly. This little layout could easily be turned into a module and made a part of a greater layout. In fact, one of our members have constructed his own version and done exactly that.
