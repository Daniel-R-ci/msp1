Modules and module railroading

Both for homes and meetings

The little layout in this picture is a small switching layout (still under construction), designed for the modelers who also enjoys the task of switching freight cars to the correct destination. The modeler who built this little layout have devised an operating scheme that takes about ninety minutes to complete, but can also be broken down into shorter 5-15 minute tasks.

This makes it a great little home layout for one who doesn't have much space, but it's constructed to also be able to bring to module meetings where it becomes part of a much larger plan.

When your big dream isn't feasible

Many nodelers dream of having a big layout and being able to really let their trains stretch out. Often that dream isn't feasible due to space constraints, time, and not least due to the financial aspect.

As a part of a module group, you can contribute to a bigger layout as time and money allows. You still own your own modules, your own trains, but we bring them together at meetings to create something that none of us could have made on our own.

Learning from each other

These days you can learn a lot of different skills by watching Youtube channels (and there are quite a lot of good ones out there, check or resources page for some), but there are few substitutes from seeing in real life how others have come up with different solutions to various aspects.

None of us can be an expert of everything, but there are always people there willing to show how they have done and give a helping hand when needed. Maybe you are thinking of something no one has done before, but then you have a "think tank" of friends to help you brainstorm and try out your plans.

There is also little bit of healthy peer preasure, you want to have something new to show at the next meeting. Maybe planting those trees behind the tracks that you thought of for so long?

What to do with modules

What do you do then when you have built your module and modules and bring them to a meeting? Well, that is in i big part up to you and what you want.

Some wants to run trains in a realistic fashion, something called operations. There are many different levels to this, some construct a time table and run trains with a fast clock. Others use different kinds of cards and lists to make sure every freight car gets to a specified location. It doesn't have to be harder than an instruction like "Take the empty coal hoppers to the mine, exchange them with loaded cars and return to yard with the loaded cars". The first time at a meeting that focuses on operations, there is always someone there to guide you and help you get started.

Others find operations stressfull, or not just interesting, and just want to see trains running through different scenes. Those meetings exist too, or a meeting can combine operations with what is often called "free running".

Other meetings or shows exist for the purpose of showing the hobby to the public. A club can choose to participate in a show or a fair to let visitors see trains running and to explain the concept of modules.

More than just trains

Building modules isn't just about trains and "playing with them". It can also be an outlet for creativity, arts, electronics, history and much more. Some modelers have duplicated memories from long gone eras, like their childhood village. In this Youtube clip from our Youtube Channel, you can see not just trains but also ships moving and actually passing a lock to reach inland destination, a tribute to the importance of naval shipping both in history and present days.
